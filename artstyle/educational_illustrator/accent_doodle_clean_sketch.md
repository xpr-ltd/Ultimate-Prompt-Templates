# Clean Sketch-Accent Doodle Style Standard

The clean sketch-accent doodle style is a lightweight companion illustration style used to support the book’s primary modern narrative educational illustration system. It should feel playful, intelligent, notebook-like, and expressive without appearing messy, childish, or disconnected from the main visual identity of the book. The purpose of this style is to create moments of humor, interaction, quick explanation, emotional reaction, or visual breathing space while maintaining overall artistic coherence.

The style should resemble:

* quick creative notebook sketches
* polished brainstorming doodles
* playful educational annotations
* visual thinking in progress

It should NOT resemble:

* chaotic comic scribbles
* crude stick figures
* exaggerated meme drawings
* toddler cartoon styles
* highly satirical comic strips

---

## Purpose & Usage Rules

This style is secondary and supportive.

Use ONLY for:

* activity boxes
* playful reflection sections
* humorous reactions
* margin notes
* tiny visual metaphors
* quick process explanations
* small visual reminders
* thought bubbles
* mini “builder notes”
* simple object demonstrations
* notebook-style instructional moments

Do NOT use for:

* chapter opening illustrations
* emotional scenes
* futuristic world scenes
* inspirational moments
* storytelling centerpieces
* historical timeline scenes
* full-page illustrations

The doodle style must never visually overpower the main illustration style.

---

## Rendering Style

###Line Work

* thin-to-medium clean digital linework
* slightly loose hand-drawn feeling
* controlled imperfection allowed
* lines should feel human and energetic

Avoid:

* shaky chaotic strokes
* overly polished vector perfection
* thick comic outlines

---

### Coloring

Two acceptable approaches:

#### Option A — Monochrome Accent
* black, charcoal, or dark brown linework
* minimal grayscale accents
* white or lightly textured background

#### Option B — Limited Soft Color
* same linework as above
* small restrained color accents from the main palette
* muted highlight colors only

Recommended:

* warm oranges
* soft blues
* muted greens
* pale yellows

Avoid:

* saturated rainbow palettes
* neon colors
* heavy rendering

---

## Character Styling

Characters must remain visually related to the primary style.

### Character Features

* same face structure logic
* same hairstyles
* same clothing identity
* same emotional personality

Simplifications allowed:

* fewer details
* simplified hands
* simplified clothing folds
* simplified facial rendering

---

### Expressions

Expressions should be:

* exaggerated slightly for readability
* humorous
* emotionally clear
* energetic

Good emotions for doodles:

* confusion
* excitement
* surprise
* thinking
* frustration
* curiosity
* “aha!” moments

Avoid:

* extreme slapstick distortion
* grotesque exaggeration
* overly sarcastic tone

---

## Environment Styling

Backgrounds should remain:

* minimal
* lightly suggested
* uncluttered

Use:

* notebook margins
* floating objects
* tiny props
* simple symbolic scenery

Avoid:

* full cinematic environments
* detailed perspective scenes
* complex rendering

---

## Composition Standards

The doodles should feel:

* quick to understand
* visually light
* supportive of reading flow

They should occupy:

* small-to-medium visual space
* sidebars
* corners
* activity regions
* reflection areas

Avoid:

* dominating page composition
* interrupting emotional pacing

---

## Educational Function

Each doodle should serve at least one purpose:

* explain
* reinforce
* simplify
* entertain
* guide attention
* create emotional connection

The reader should feel:

> “This book is talking with me, not just teaching me.”

---

## Visual Coherence Rules

To avoid psychological or stylistic disconnection:

* maintain the same emotional warmth as the main style
* maintain consistent character identity
* reuse visual motifs from the primary style
* use the same overall color philosophy when color is included
* ensure transitions between styles feel natural and intentional

The doodle style should feel like:

> a lighter sketchbook extension of the main world — not a completely different universe.

---

## Overall Emotional Tone

The clean sketch-accent doodles should communicate:

* curiosity
* playfulness
* experimentation
* imagination
* friendly humor
* creative thinking

The style should make readers feel:

> “It’s okay to think, sketch, try, and explore ideas.”
